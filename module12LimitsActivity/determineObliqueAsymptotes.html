Our last case, when the degree of the numerator is larger than the degree of the denominator, is much trickier. Since the numerator is larger, we can perform divide the polynomials to get some quotient (another, smaller polynomial) and then a remainder. In Calculus, you’ll learn how to evaluate these limits without needing to divide the polynomials. For our case, we will simplify the issue by making sure we can use synthetic division to perform the polynomial division. The quotient will result in an oblique asymptote, or an asymptote that is not horizontal/vertical.
